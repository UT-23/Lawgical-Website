# KodyTechnolab Interaction Analysis

## Purpose
This document captures the interaction language to borrow for a premium law firm website without copying the brand itself.

## What makes it feel premium
- Dark, high-contrast presentation
- Large typographic hierarchy
- Interactive service cards that reveal images on hover
- Expanding location panels on the contact page
- Segmented service choices instead of generic dropdowns
- Subtle motion with strong visual feedback
- Clean spacing and polished transitions

## Patterns worth adapting for a law firm
### 1. Service image-reveal cards
- Default: dark card with large number, title, and short copy
- Hover: background image fades in behind text
- Use for practice areas, services, or case categories

### 2. Expanding location panels
- Collapsed state: thin vertical tabs
- Expanded state: office image, address, and contact info
- Use for office locations on the contact page

### 3. Segmented service selectors
- Replace plain select menus with clickable chips
- Use for service inquiry type, practice area selection, or consultation type

### 4. Strong hero hierarchy
- Large headline
- One animated phrase or rotating service focus
- Minimal but elegant motion

### 5. Premium contact treatment
- Floating icons inside inputs
- Dark surfaces with subtle glow on focus
- Clear visual feedback for selected options

## Law-firm adaptation notes
- Keep motion restrained and professional
- Avoid playful or startup-like effects
- Prefer trust, clarity, and authority over spectacle
- Use hover reveals for premium feel, but never at the cost of readability

## Best-fit sections
- Homepage hero
- Practice areas
- Attorney team
- Contact page
- Office locations
- Testimonials
- Case results
